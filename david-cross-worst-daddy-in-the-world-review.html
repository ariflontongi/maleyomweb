David Cross, Worst Daddy in the World, review: seething show

August 27, 2024
post

Similar to the way in which Stewart Lee’s seminal mid-2000s show 90s Comedian railed at Christian hypocrisy, culminating in a hallucinatory, visually vile, transgressive encounter with Jesus, David Cross’s Worst Daddy in the World show ends with the American comic recreating the virgin birth in messy physical detail, before he violently but apologetically subverts the course of Western history by abducting the adolescent Christ.

Though less playfully shocking than Lee’s deliberately provocative showstopper, Cross, star of both television series Arrested Development and Mr Show, has likewise built his proceeding 90 minutes to this sacrilegious pressure release.

A liberal, atheist New Yorker, the 59-year-old of Jewish heritage nevertheless hails from scripture-saturated Georgia in the Deep South, where the roll-back of education, as well as the rights of women and African-Americans, is currently taking place. 

Deploying his six-year-old daughter to fulminate against the pro-life movement, Cross measures his own bad actions against those of God’s: who is the worst daddy in the world? At first, he seems content in his misanthropy, expressing hatred of the hipster, 30-something fathers whom he’s forced to make stilted conversation with in Brooklyn playgrounds.

Incredulous that anyone could serve jail time for murdering their toddler when casual negligence would be just as effective (and less incriminating), he incrementally shifts from his own failings to those of a pro-life God. 

While the pacing can be a little slow overall, the night’s best routine sees Cross shift from Ron DeSantis’s legislative rewriting of Rosa Parks’s story in Florida schools to a hilariously demented equivocation of Hitler’s Final Solution. But it is then cratered by an unwise bit of roleplay with the audience, with not one but two volunteers making a mess of things. Happily, Worst Daddy survives this car crash to end with Cross irreverently rewriting the Old Testament to excellent effect.
