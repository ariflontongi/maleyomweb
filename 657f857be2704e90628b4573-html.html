Do the Chiefs have anything to worry about? Travis Kelce leaves the game against the Pats injured

August 11, 2024
post

In Week 15, the Kansas City Chiefs faced a setback as star tight end Travis Kelce left the field with an apparent elbow injury. The incident occurred in the fourth quarter when Kelce made an 11-yard catch for a first down. Despite wincing in pain, the tight end managed to finish the game.

Chiefs head coach Andy Reid provided an injury update after the game, emphasizing that the injury was a "stinger," minimizing concerns about its severity. While Kelce soldiered on, wide receiver Skyy Moore faced a more serious issue with a swollen knee, requiring further tests.

Kelce's injury comes amid a season with occasional health concerns, including a bone bruise that sidelined him in Week 1 and an ankle injury in October that affected his practice sessions.

The Chiefs, already coping with injuries to left tackle Donovan Smith and running back Isiah Pacheco, navigated Sunday's game with additional challenges. Cornerback Joshua Williams was absent due to illness, becoming a last-minute addition to the injury report.

Andy Reid is more concerned about Skyy Moore

Despite these hurdles, the Chiefs secured a 27-17 victory over the New England Patriots, thus snapping a two-game losing streak.

Reid's post-game injury report highlighted Moore's knee issue as the primary concern. Moore, a second-year wide receiver, left the game due to the injury, with additional details expected in subsequent updates.

The Chiefs, with a 10-4 record, are now turning their attention to the final stretch of the regular season. Kelce's health will be closely monitored as the team seeks to maintain its momentum heading into the playoffs.
