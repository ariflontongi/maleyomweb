What Happened To Gorilla Glue Girl?

August 18, 2024
post

In early 2021, a story about a woman who used Gorilla Glue instead of hair spray went viral, capturing the attention of millions worldwide. This woman, Tessica Brown, became known as “Gorilla Glue Girl” after sharing her sticky situation on social media. The incident not only led to a public outcry of support and concern but also raised questions about product safety and consumer awareness. This article delves into the full story of what happened to Gorilla Glue Girl, her journey to recovery, and the aftermath of her viral fame.

The Viral Incident

It all started when Tessica Brown, a 40-year-old woman from Louisiana, took to TikTok to share her hair dilemma. In a video that quickly went viral, Brown explained that she had used Gorilla Glue adhesive spray on her hair after running out of her usual hair spray. She realized her mistake when her hair remained firmly in place for over a month, despite numerous attempts to wash it out.

Understanding the Product

Gorilla Glue is a polyurethane-based adhesive designed for household and industrial use, known for its strength and permanence. It is not intended for use on the body or hair, as clearly stated on the product’s warning label. Brown’s predicament highlighted the importance of reading and understanding product labels.

Public Reaction and Support

The public’s reaction to Brown’s situation was a mix of shock, sympathy, and, for some, amusement. However, as the seriousness of her condition became apparent, many people expressed genuine concern and offered support. Celebrities and influencers shared her story, and the hashtag #GorillaGlueGirl trended on social media platforms.

Medical Intervention

After trying home remedies without success, Brown sought medical treatment. She visited the emergency room, where healthcare professionals attempted to remove the adhesive, causing further discomfort. The situation seemed dire until a Beverly Hills plastic surgeon, Dr. Michael Obeng, offered to perform a procedure to remove the glue free of charge.

The Procedure

Dr. Obeng’s procedure involved a custom-developed solvent to dissolve the Gorilla Glue, combined with aloe vera, olive oil, and acetone. The process took four hours and was a success, finally freeing Brown from her hair predicament without causing major damage to her scalp or hair.

Life After the Glue

Following the procedure, Brown experienced relief and gratitude. She shared updates on her recovery and expressed her thanks to Dr. Obeng and the public for their support. Brown’s story served as a cautionary tale about the misuse of products and the power of social media to rally community support.

Legal Considerations

There was speculation about whether Brown would pursue legal action against Gorilla Glue. However, she clarified that she did not intend to sue the company, acknowledging that the misuse of the product was her own mistake.

Impact on Gorilla Glue

The company behind Gorilla Glue responded to the incident by reiterating the importance of using their products as directed and expressing relief that Brown received medical help. The incident brought unexpected attention to the brand and prompted discussions about product labeling and consumer education.

Media Coverage and Interviews

Brown’s story was covered by major news outlets, and she participated in several interviews, sharing her experience in more detail. The media coverage highlighted the dangers of viral challenges and the importance of using products responsibly.

Personal Growth and Opportunities

Despite the ordeal, Brown found a silver lining. She gained a significant following on social media and used her platform to advocate for product safety. She also launched a line of merchandise and received opportunities to collaborate with beauty brands.

Health and Hair Recovery

Post-procedure, Brown focused on her health and hair recovery. She documented her journey on social media, offering updates on the condition of her hair and scalp, and sharing the products and treatments that helped her recover.

Public Education and Awareness

The incident with Gorilla Glue Girl underscored the need for public education on product usage. It sparked conversations about the responsibilities of manufacturers and consumers when it comes to product safety and proper use.

Philanthropic Efforts

Brown used her newfound platform to engage in philanthropic efforts. She donated a portion of the funds raised through a GoFundMe campaign to Dr. Obeng’s foundation, which provides reconstructive surgery for people in need.

Continued Social Media Presence
