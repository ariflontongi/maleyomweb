Months of rainy season in Nigeria

August 15, 2024
post

The raining season in Nigeria does not start in the same months in different parts of Nigeria. The climate in the Northern part of the country is different from the climate in Southern Nigeria. Also, rainfall distribution in Nigeria differs from the South to the North. In this post, we will tell you about the months of the rainy season in Nigeria. Keep reading to learn more about these differences.

Just like every other country in West Africa and the tropic lands, there are only two major seasons in Nigeria namely:

  • dry season
  • raining season

Here we will be talking only about the raining season.

The rainy season is the planting season in our country. If we are to talk about Nigeria in general, it begins in the late weeks of the month of February and ends in October. The raining season is influenced and controlled by the wind from the South Atlantic Ocean known as the Tropical Maritime airmass or South West Wind.

How many seasons do we have in Nigeria?

However, the raining season does not begin in February in every part of Nigeria because the Tropical Maritime airmass takes a long time to cover the whole country. The rain begins in the southern part of Nigeria around February and does not reach the northern part of the country until around June. So, the raining season begins a little bit later than February in the Northern parts of Nigeria.

The Tropical maritime airmass also determines the rainfall distribution in Nigeria. In the Southern part of the country, the raining season begins in the late weeks of February/early weeks of March and ends in October.

The raining seasons in most parts of Southern Nigeria peaks in June with heavy rainfalls around this time. The raining season in most parts of Northern Nigeria begins in the month of June/July and ends in September. Most parts of Northern Nigeria experience the peak of the rainy season during the month of August, although not as high as the heavy rainfall experienced in Southern Nigeria.
